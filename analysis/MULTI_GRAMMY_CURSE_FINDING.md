### Christopher Cross: The One-Year Peak

**The Perfect Storm (1981):**
- Won FOUR major Grammys same night:
  - Best New Artist
  - Album of the Year
  - Record of the Year  
  - Song of the Year
- Unprecedented sweep - one of only 2 artists ever to win all four

**Post-Peak Reality:**
- Average position declined **+25.6 points**
- Never matched that peak year commercially
- Critical acclaim peak = commercial peak = unsustainable

### Natalie Cole: The Unicorn

**Only Multi-Grammy Winner to Escape Commercially:**
- Won BNA in 1976

**What makes her different:**
- Long gap between wins (16 years)
- Career resurgence vs same-year peak
- Tribute album to Nat King Cole (built-in audience)

**Statistical status:** Exception that proves the rule (1 out of 66 BNA winners)

## üî¨ Why This Matters

### For Understanding the Curse

The commercial curse is **NOT about lack of talent or industry recognition:**
- The Beatles were cursed (most influential band ever)
- Christopher Cross swept all four major Grammys (still cursed)
- Winning more Grammys doesn't prevent commercial decline

**The curse is structural, not qualitative:**
- Peak timing effect (pre-Grammy = rising star focus)
- Regression to mean (impossible to sustain breakthrough momentum)
- Volume paradox (more output = natural variation in success)
